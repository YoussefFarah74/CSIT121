Slow Cooker Taco Soup

Recipe by Janeen Barlow

Recipe ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 (16 ounce) can chili beans, with liquid
  • 1 (15 ounce) can kidney beans with liquid
  • 1 (15 ounce) can whole kernel corn, with liquid
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans peeled and diced tomatoes
  • 1 (4 ounce) can diced green chile peppers
  • 1 (1.25 ounce) package taco seasoning mix

Recipe steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. In a medium skillet, cook the ground beef until browned over medium heat. Drain, and set aside.
  3. Place the ground beef, onion, chili beans, kidney beans, corn, tomato sauce, water, diced tomatoes, green chile peppers and taco seasoning mix in a slow cooker.
  4. Mix to blend, and cook on Low setting for 8 hours.

The slow cooker taco soup is my favorite soup for a number of reasons.

First, it has an enticingly rich and savory taste. Every mouthful of the ground beef dish dances on my taste senses as a delicious medley of savory flavors created by the addition of onions, bell peppers, garlic, and ground beef. Second, the ease of making this soup into a slow cooker increases its appeal. I enjoy how simple it is to prepare since I can just throw everything into the slow cooker in the morning and let it stew and mellow out its flavors all day. It is a sheer delight to arrive at the calming scent of the soup boiling.
